**Corpo (~420 palavras):**

There's one rule behind every piece we add to the Weekly Edit: it has to work at least three ways. If it can only do one job, it doesn't make the list. That single test is how a small rotation of pieces ends up covering a whole week without ever feeling repetitive.

### What "three ways" actually means
A great piece flexes across moments. Take a high-waist plaid skirt: tuck in a fitted knit for the office, swap to a relaxed tee and sneakers for the weekend, then add a satin cami and a heel for dinner. Same skirt, three completely different energies. That's the test — and it's why versatile pieces quietly become the ones you reach for most.

### Fabric does half the work
The pieces that pass the three-way test almost always share one thing: fabric that moves. Mesh detailing, a mix-media panel, a fit-and-flare cut that holds its shape — these are the details that let one item read "polished" in the morning and "easy" by evening without you changing a thing.

### Build around one statement, keep the rest quiet
Our styling formula on the Weekly Edit page is simple: one statement piece, one basic, one finishing detail. The statement piece carries the look; the basics let it breathe; the finishing detail (a gold accent, a structured bag) makes it feel intentional. Get that balance right and getting dressed takes minutes.

### This week's pieces that pass
This week's edit leans into easy, feminine pieces with a little movement — mesh tops, a versatile plaid skirt, and fit-and-flare classics that flatter without fuss. Each one earns its place by doing more than one job.
